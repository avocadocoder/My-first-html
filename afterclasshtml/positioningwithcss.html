STICKY: I will stick when you scroll

In web development, positioning refers to how elements are placed on a page using CSS.

The most common values are static, relative, absolute, fixed, and sticky.

For example, relative lets you move an element slightly from its normal position, while absolute positions it based on its nearest positioned parent.

Fixed keeps an element locked to the screen (like a navbar), and sticky behaves like a mix of relative and fixed depending on scroll.

Understanding positioning is key to building layouts that feel structured and responsive.
