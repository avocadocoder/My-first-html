Nebulas

What is a nebula?

A nebula is a vast, interstellar cloud of dust, hydrogen, helium, and other ionized gases found in space, often acting as a nursery for new stars. Ranging in size from a few light-years to hundreds, these "stellar nurseries" form stars when gravity causes the gas and dust to collapse and heat up. Some, however, are remnants of dead or dying stars.
