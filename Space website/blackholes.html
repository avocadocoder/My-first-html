Blackholes

What are blackholes?

A black hole is a region in space with gravity so intensely strong that nothing, not even light, can escape from it. Formed when massive stars collapse at the end of their life cycles, these objects concentrate immense amounts of matter into a tiny, dense area. They are defined by an "event horizon," the boundary of no return, and a central "singularity".
